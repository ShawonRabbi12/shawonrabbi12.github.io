What Is GEO? Generative Engine Optimization Guide

By Shawon Rabbi · Technical SEO, AEO & GEO

Quick answer

GEO, short for Generative Engine Optimization, is about making a website's content easy for generative AI tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Copilot — to find, understand, trust, and quote when they put together an answer. It's the AI-era counterpart to traditional SEO.

What does GEO stand for?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. Where SEO and AEO are mostly concerned with ranking or formatting a single page, GEO asks a different question: when an AI system is generating a response, does it choose to pull from your content and credit it as a source? That depends on more than keywords — it depends on whether the AI system can read your content cleanly and trusts it enough to repeat.

How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO works toward a ranking position. GEO works toward a citation inside a generated answer, which might never show a traditional results page at all. A page can sit at the top of page one and still get skipped by an AI Overview if its structure or sourcing doesn't give the AI system enough confidence to quote it directly.

How is GEO different from AEO?

The two sit close together. AEO is mostly about formatting a single page to surface a direct answer. GEO is the wider picture: entity consistency across the web, structured data, content freshness, and explicit signals like an llms.txt file — everything that affects whether an AI system trusts a source enough to cite it anywhere, not just on one page. For the formatting side of this, see what AEO actually involves.

What actually helps with GEO?

Structured data

Person, Organization, and Article schema hand an AI system explicit, machine-readable facts instead of forcing it to guess from context.

Entity consistency

Use the same name, title, and description everywhere you appear — your website, LinkedIn, and any other profile. A consistent identity is easier for an AI system to recognize and trust.

Why this matters more than it sounds like it should

AI systems often cross-check a claim against multiple places before repeating it. If your title reads differently on three different platforms, that's a small but real signal of inconsistency working against you.

An llms.txt file

A short, plain-text file at the root of your site that summarizes what it's about and points to the key pages. It costs almost nothing to add and is becoming a normal part of how sites signal themselves to AI crawlers.

Clear, self-contained sections

Content that fully answers one question in one place is easier for an AI system to lift and cite accurately — the same principle covered in more depth in the AEO guide.

A solid technical SEO base

None of the above matters if a page isn't crawlable and indexable in the first place. Clean architecture and fast load times remain the foundation everything else sits on.

Frequently asked questions

What does GEO stand for?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization — the practice of making content easy for generative AI systems to find, understand, trust, and cite in their responses.

How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO optimizes for ranking in a list of search results. GEO optimizes for being selected, summarized, and cited inside an AI-generated answer, which depends more on trust signals and structured data than on ranking position alone.

What is llms.txt and do I need one?

llms.txt is a plain text file at the root of a website that gives AI crawlers a structured summary of the site and its key pages. It's an emerging convention, not yet a universal standard, but it's a low-effort signal that costs nothing to add.

Does GEO replace technical SEO?

No. GEO depends on the same foundation as SEO — a crawlable, indexable, well-structured site. Without that foundation, there's nothing for an AI system to cite in the first place.
