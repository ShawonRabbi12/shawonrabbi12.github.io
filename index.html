About

Search has split in two. There's still the classic ten blue links — but increasingly, there's also the single answer: an AI Overview, a chatbot response, a voice assistant's summary. Both are won or lost at the technical layer, before a single word of marketing copy matters.

My work sits there: site architecture, crawl efficiency, structured data, and content formatting that search engines and AI systems can parse with confidence. Less persuasion, more plumbing.

I work across three layers of the same problem — Technical SEO, AEO (Answer Engine Optimization), and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization): can a machine find your content, understand it, and trust it enough to show it?

// how a page gets found

Crawl to citation

  1. 01

    Crawl

    Search engines and AI crawlers need a clear, efficient path to your content — clean architecture, no dead ends, no wasted budget.

  2. 02

    Render

    JavaScript-heavy pages need to render the same content a crawler sees as a visitor does. Mismatches here quietly kill visibility.

  3. 03

    Index

    Being crawled isn't being indexed. Canonical signals, duplicate content, and indexation directives decide what actually enters the index.

  4. 04

    Rank & Cite

    Once indexed, content competes for two outcomes: ranking in search results, or being cited directly inside an AI-generated answer.

  5. 05

    Monitor

    Search Console, server logs, and AI citation tracking close the loop — so the next round of work is based on evidence, not guesswork.
