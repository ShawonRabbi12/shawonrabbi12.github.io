What Is AEO? Answer Engine Optimization Explained

By Shawon Rabbi · Technical SEO, AEO & GEO

Quick answer

AEO, short for Answer Engine Optimization, is about shaping content so it can be lifted out and shown as a direct answer — by Google's AI Overviews, by a voice assistant, or by a chatbot like ChatGPT — instead of just sitting in a list of results waiting to be clicked.

What does AEO stand for?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. It covers anything you do to get a specific piece of your content chosen as the answer to a question, rather than just one entry in a list of search results. Featured snippets, People Also Ask boxes, voice assistant replies, and AI-generated summaries all fall under this umbrella.

How is AEO different from traditional SEO?

Same content, different outcome

Traditional SEO gets a page ranking well enough that someone clicks through and reads it to find their answer. AEO goes a step further: it shapes a specific section of that page so the answer itself can be lifted out and shown directly, sometimes right inside the search results and sometimes inside an AI-generated response, before anyone clicks anything.

They work together, not against each other

These two aren't rival strategies. A page that already ranks well because of solid technical SEO is also the page most likely to get pulled into an AI Overview or a featured snippet. Think of AEO as a layer built on top of SEO, not a replacement for it.

How is AEO different from GEO?

AEO and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) cover overlapping ground but aren't quite the same thing. AEO is mostly about format — writing a section so it resolves a question completely enough to be lifted as a direct answer. GEO is the bigger picture: whether an AI system trusts a source enough to cite or recommend it at all, which depends on more than any single page's formatting.

How do you optimize content for AEO?

Lead with the answer

Open each section with the direct answer in plain words, then add the supporting detail underneath. Don't make a reader (or an AI system) dig through three paragraphs to find what they came for.

Use question-based headings

Phrase your headings the way people actually ask the question out loud. "How is AEO different from SEO?" matches a real search far better than something like "AEO vs. SEO: An Overview."

Resolve one idea per section

Try to fully answer a single question within one section instead of scattering the answer across the page. A complete, self-contained answer is what gets extracted and quoted.

Add structured data

Structured data turns your content into something a machine can read with confidence, not just guess at. Two types matter most for AEO:

FAQPage schema

This marks up your question-and-answer pairs explicitly, which is exactly the format voice assistants and AI Overviews are built to pull from.

Article schema

This tells search engines and AI systems who wrote the piece, when it was published, and what it's about — useful context when a system is deciding whether to trust and cite it.

Write in plain language

Short sentences and concrete wording extract far more cleanly than dense, jargon-heavy prose. If you wouldn't say it out loud to a colleague, it probably won't read well as an AI-generated answer either.

Frequently asked questions

What does AEO stand for?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization — structuring content so it can be extracted and presented as a direct answer by search engines and AI tools.

Is AEO the same as SEO?

No. SEO aims to rank a page in a list of results. AEO aims to have a specific passage of content selected as the direct answer, with or without a click-through.

Is AEO the same as GEO?

They overlap but are not identical. AEO focuses on direct-answer formats like featured snippets and voice answers. GEO is broader, covering whether AI tools trust and cite a source at all.

Do I need schema markup for AEO?

It helps. Schema markup, especially FAQPage and Article schema, gives search engines and AI systems explicit signals about what a piece of content answers.
