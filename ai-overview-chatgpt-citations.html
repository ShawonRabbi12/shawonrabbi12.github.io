How to Get Cited in AI Overviews and ChatGPT

By Shawon Rabbi · Technical SEO, AEO & GEO

Quick answer

Getting cited in AI Overviews and tools like ChatGPT comes down to three things: a page that's easy to crawl and parse, content where a single section fully answers one question, and trust signals — schema markup and consistent entity details — that give the AI system confidence to repeat what you've said.

Why isn't my content showing up in AI Overviews?

Most of the time the problem isn't the topic, it's the structure. The right information is on the page, but it's spread across several paragraphs instead of stated clearly in one place. AI systems lean toward passages that resolve a question completely on their own — that's what they can lift and present with confidence.

Does ranking on page one guarantee a citation?

No, and this trips people up. It helps, since AI systems often draw from pages that already rank well, but ranking and citation are separate outcomes. A page can hold a strong position and still get passed over if a competing page states the answer more directly or backs it with clearer structured data.

What are the concrete steps?

Lead with the answer

Put the direct answer to the obvious question in the first sentence or two of a section, then expand below it. This is the same principle behind good AEO writing.

Use question-shaped headings

Headings phrased the way people actually ask a question are easier for an AI system to match against a real query.

Add schema markup

FAQPage and Article schema give an explicit, machine-readable version of your question-and-answer pairs, instead of leaving an AI system to infer the structure on its own.

Keep entity details consistent

The same name, title, and description across your site and professional profiles reinforces a single, trustworthy identity — one of the core ideas behind GEO.

Add an llms.txt file

A short summary file aimed at AI crawlers, listing your key pages and what the site covers. Low effort, growing adoption.

Fix the technical basics first

Everything above depends on this being right. Two things to check before anything else:

Crawlability

If a crawler can't reach a page cleanly, none of its content gets seen at all, no matter how well it's written.

Page speed

Slow-loading pages get crawled less often and rendered less reliably, which quietly reduces how often they're considered as a source.

Frequently asked questions

Why isn't my content showing up in AI Overviews?

Usually because the answer to the likely question is buried inside a long paragraph instead of stated clearly and early, or because the page lacks the structured data that would make the content easier to parse with confidence.

Does ranking on page one guarantee an AI citation?

No. Ranking helps because AI systems often pull from well-ranking pages, but a page can rank well and still be skipped if its structure or sourcing doesn't give the AI system enough confidence to cite it directly.

Do I need to publish on multiple platforms to get cited?

It helps. When the same core information appears consistently across a website, LinkedIn, and other professional profiles, AI systems treat that consistency as a trust signal.
